The electric two-wheeler market is set for exponential growth, transitioning from a niche to a mainstream segment. This rapid expansion represents both a massive opportunity and a significant disruption risk for established players focused on traditional engines.

EV Adoption Hurdles

Successfully navigating the EV transition requires overcoming several key obstacles that impact widespread consumer adoption.

1

Charging Infrastructure

A significant lack of charging stations, especially in rural and semi-urban areas, creates range anxiety for potential buyers.

2

High Upfront Cost

Despite subsidies, the initial purchase price of EVs remains higher than their ICE counterparts, a major barrier in a price-sensitive market.

3

Supply Chain Reliance

Dependence on imported components like batteries and semiconductors creates vulnerability to geopolitical tensions and price volatility.
